Set apart from the rest of Japan both geographically and culturally, Okinawa Prefecture consists of 160 islands where nature abounds and laid-back, tropical ambiance prevails. Discover pristine beaches and excellent diving, as well as historic artifacts from World War II battles, unique museums, and a deep-rooted culture. Okinawa Island, the largest of the prefecture’s 40 inhabited islands, has the capital city of Naha, the gateway to the rest of the coral-fringed islands. The city’s highlight is the 14th-century Shurijo Castle. Learn more about the ancient Ryukyu culture at the Okinawa Prefectural Museum & Art Museum. Visit pottery shops in the Tsuboya area, eat, and interact with locals along International Street. Tour the Peace Memorial Museum on the southern part of the island for artifacts and testimonials related to Okinawa’s World War II battles. Appreciate nature on Okinawa’s other islands, embark on a whale-watching cruise off Zamami Island, witness the earliest blossoming of Japan’s famous pink cherry blossoms or attend one of the many colorful flower festivals held throughout the beautiful islands of Okinawa.

Kyūshū, Japan's southern and westernmost main island, is arguably its warmest and most beautiful, with active volcanic peaks, rocky, lush, and near-tropical coastlines, and great onsen (hot springs) virtually everywhere. History and legend were made here: Jōmon ruins, Shintō's sun goddess, wealthy trading ports, cloistered foreigners, samurai rebels, and one of the earth's greatest wartime tragedies all loom large. Kyushu is the third-largest island of Japan’s five main islands. In the past, it has been known as Kyuukoku (“Nine Countries”), Chinzei (“West of the Pacified Area”), and Tsukushi-no-Shima (“Island of Tsukushi”). As an early center of Japanese civilization, Kyushu offers many historic treasures, modern cities, and natural beauty. Kyushu and the near islands are divided into the seven prefectures of Fukuoka, Kagoshima, Kumamoto, Miyazaki, Nagasaki, Ōita, and Saga. In addition, the region includes the portion of the Ryukyus within Okinawa prefecture. With its wild and omnipresent nature, rich history and culture, friendly and welcoming inhabitants, and of course delicious local specialties, Kyushu Island has plenty of treasures to be discovered.
